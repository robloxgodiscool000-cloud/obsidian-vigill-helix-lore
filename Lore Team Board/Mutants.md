# Mutants

> [!summary] Overview
> Mutants are the surviving victims of the Sanguine Mist. Humans and their offspring permanently altered by the bioweapons deployed during The Greatest War. While fully integrated into StellarGen society, they are heavily discriminated against and are often the targets of violence from the populations of unaffected humans.

## Origins
The mutant population was born from the ashes of **The Greatest War**. When the Sanguine Mist blanketed a majority of the globe, millions died. However, a percentage of the population survived the exposure, however their DNA was rewritten by the self-replicating bioweapons. The mutation process is not uniform; the Mist reacts differently to individual genetics, resulting in a wide spectrum of physical and biological alterations. 

## Physiology and Traits
Because the Mist encompasses multiple, unique strains, mutations vary wildly. However, the dominant strain which many Mutants have been affected by, either through contact or offspring, exhibits the following changes on the human body:

* The Sanguine Mist strain destabilises and rewrites foundational skeletal and muscular templates. This allows for immense variation in height and mass, radical shifts in bipedal posture, and the development of supplemental appendages (such as prehensile tails, extra digits, or secondary arms).
* The integumentary system (skin, hair, and nails) undergoes severe modification as a biological defence mechanism. Manifestations include dermal plating, reptilian or mammalian-like coverings, spontaneous bioluminescence, and radical shifts in pigmentation beyond the baseline human spectrum.

## Social Standings in Vigill Helix
### StellarGen
Under the corporate structure of StellarGen, mutants are legally recognised as sentient beings. This provides mutants with the same rights, opportunities and possibilities as regular humans have within the organisation. Discrimination and harassment is strictly outlawed to protect the peace within StellarGen's companies.
## Remaining Nations
**The Remaining Nations** exhibit divergent policies regarding mutant populations. While several nations align with StellarGen’s example, legally recognising mutants as sentient, autonomous agents possessing free will, others enforce zero-tolerance doctrines, ruthlessly exterminating any mutated individuals discovered within their borders.
## AWOL Corps
Much like the **Remaining Nations**, the decentralised nature of the AWOL Corps results in highly fractured ideological stances on the mutant population. While certain global cells operate under strict extermination protocols driven by deep-seated hostility, others adopt a ruthless, utilitarian approach. These pragmatic cells classify mutants not as equals, but as an operational necessity or exotic, expendable assets for specialised tasks. Of course, AWOL Corps Cells rise and fall continually; opinions shift wildly across groups, with some Cells being made up of only mutants.
## Rose Accord
As a collective entity, the **Remaining Nations** alliance, formalised under the **Rose Accord**, maintains a strictly neutral stance regarding the mutant populace. Despite the fiercely divergent ideologies of its individual member states, the Accord's operational doctrine is driven by cold pragmatism. Their ranks integrate both baseline humans and mutants, prioritising operational efficiency to achieve their primary directives: preserving geopolitical stability in the aftermath of the **Greatest War** and countering StellarGen's expanding hegemony.
## Social Dynamics and Discrimination
Despite being essential to the global economy, mutants face severe systemic prejudice. 
* Many baseline, regular humans hold an irrational, prejudiced belief that mutants passively exhale or spread the Sanguine Mist, leading to intense social friction and outright hostility in integrated societies, which often escalates to progroms, spurring mutant populations to travel to StellarGen-NOVA Sites for refuge as **Observers.**
